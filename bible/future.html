After a long trip of moving... again... Phil decides to take Hope and only Hope on a "business trip" with him. They go around scamming people for money, Phil using Hope's "sickness" as a ploy to gain affection, when they suddenly come across a magician living in his van. Oh no, the magician doesn't like that one bit. He follows them back to their "house" that they're renting out and finds Aurora hanging around with Axolotl. Hope eventually punches out the magician, because her one solution to everything is to punch it, but the magician will now follow them for a few episodes to try to exact his revenge on hope, the better magician, until Phillip pins the cult onto him momentarily.

Hope-less Romantic

You guys... there's actually someone in this world as dark and as twisted as Hope is. While Hope was out getting groceries and "accidentally" slipping into the sports section, she ran into the most gorgeous girl buying mouth guards and boxing gloves. They hit it off and became sparring partners for a date. Vvoyd wants to "help" Hope in her pursuit of this insane romance in his own insane, chaotic way. Aurora, of course, is completely against the idea. She says that Hope would never be able to love anyone with the way that she is--everyone in the family knows of her "condition" by this point--and that she would be better just hiding away. While Hope is originally totally against the idea, she learns that Vvoyd's chaos might actually be a good thing when his crazy idea to collect flowers from the bottom of a lake actually go over well in the overall romantic scheme of things. Meanwhile, Harmony is off collecting her own romances... with an arch-angel's kid with the ability to teleport who is dead-set on killing Vvoyd and Hope. But you don't understand, he's the only one who gets Christian mythology like Harmony does!! He doesn't make fun of her for liking angels so much, in fact, he loves them as much as she does. While the magician guy was more of a joke, this kid actually means business. He's a serious threat.

Overall Arcs, Themes, Etc...

As for the structure of the show, I'm thinking of a lot of hints dropping regarding what the heck is going on. Vvoyd doesn't know exactly what happened, but someone certainly does. and they're not saying something about it. This is also a mystery show... lots of mysteries. The show moves in a linear fashion, where each episode follows each other, and there is a story arc over the course of the first season.

As for this story arc... they're being hunted down by a cult that believes they own Hope simply because they're the ones that put Vvoyd inside of her, and eventually they catch up and the frosts have to deal with them.

Vvoyd and Hope have to learn to live with each other. Hope and Harmony go through some personal struggles as well, having a falling out as Hope doesn't approve of ANY of Harmony's many boyfriends--especially not the one that turns out to be a demon-slaying angel that wants to hunt down and murder Hope/Vvoyd.

As for Aurora, she will have a villainous arc, always trying to get Hope alone or trying to leave her behind when the family moves. We may have an episode dedicated to Hope and Aurora trying to work out their differences, but in the end, Aurora remains superficial.

Phillip wants nothing more but to protect his daughter, even if it means lying and upheaving them from their home every few weeks without any explanation. He knows what's up, and his arc may include him finally coming to terms with his more than shady past... Phillip, you dirty, dirty, dawg.

The themes of the show, like I said before, are family conflict, fighting your inner demons, justice... um. probably some other stuff. standing up for yourself and others who can't stand up for themselves. having a good heart even when your methods are crazy. not judging a book by its cover. all that good jazz.

The story world includes many, many places in America. we got big cities, mountain towns, suburbs, swamps, deserts, forests, endless fields, probably some magic place.
